How do we learn to make sense of the world?

At birth, brains face a complex computational challenge. Without prior experience or instruction, brains must convert streams of high-dimensional sensory input into knowledge and adaptive action. I study the algorithmic principles underlying this feat: what learning mechanisms drive the development of perception and cognition, what role experience plays in developing knowledge, and how intrinsic curiosity scaffolds learning.

About

To address questions about how learning and cognition develop, I work at the intersection of cognitive science and artificial intelligence (AI).

Using tools from AI, I formalize theories from psychology as agent-based models that use deep neural networks to perform the same tasks as humans and animals. I situate computational neuroscience models in virtual animal bodies to test how different components of embodied systems (e.g., neural mechanisms, the environment, body morphologies) drive the behavior of an entire organism. By simulating the development of intelligence with AI, I aim to build predictive, integrative, and embodied models for psychology and neuroscience.

Research focus

I build embodied computational models that connect neural mechanisms, experience, and the environment to explain how perception and cognition emerge.

Embodied agents

Virtual animal bodies let us test how morphology and sensorimotor loops shape cognition.

Developmental learning

Deep neural networks are used to model how perception and knowledge evolve over time.

Curiosity & exploration

Intrinsic motivation helps explain how learners seek information without external instruction.

Teaching

Courses that build practical and critical skills for students.

  • Data Fluency

    Data Fluency

    Data is big. Data is everywhere. How can we possibly be expected to keep up in a world full of data, much of which is data about ourselves? This class provides fundamental skills for the 21st century: understanding data, extracting knowledge from data, generating predictions from data, and presenting data.

  • Introduction to Virtual Reality

    Introduction to Virtual Reality

    Virtual Reality (VR) is an exciting new technology that allows users to experience rich, realistic, and interactive virtual worlds. VR has applications in fields as diverse as medicine, education, military training, trauma recovery, and artificial intelligence. In this course, students will learn the foundational skills needed to build virtual reality environments using Unity.
